9. Interview Questions and Answers


Q1: What is CQRS?

Answer:

CQRS is a design pattern that separates read operations (queries) from write operations (commands) so they can scale and evolve independently.

Q2: Why use CQRS?

Answer:

To improve scalability, performance, and separation of concerns when read and write workloads differ significantly.

Q3: What problem does CQRS solve?

Answer:

It solves the problem where reads and writes have different scaling needs and different optimization requirements.

Q4: What is the downside of CQRS?

Answer:

It increases complexity, infrastructure overhead, and introduces eventual consistency.

Q5: When should CQRS be used?

Answer:

Use CQRS in:

High read/write scale systems
Distributed systems
Event-driven systems

Not for simple CRUD apps.

Q6: CQRS vs CRUD?

Answer:

CRUD uses one model for reads/writes.

CQRS separates:

command model
query model

This improves scalability but increases complexity.
